TinyPumper Partner Program

This is built for folks who are already out in the field: installing gear, running service calls, and keeping wells online for their clients.

You bring the boots on the ground. We bring the tech. You get paid on both sides.

How You Make Money

There are two ways you earn as a partner. The first is a recurring cut. The second is your own service work.

Some partners love the recurring side. They refer clients, collect their 20%, and let us handle the rest. Others prefer to be more hands-on: running installs, charging for drive time, troubleshooting, and providing ongoing support directly to their clients. That's where you can create a lot of additional value and charge accordingly. Either way works. You decide how involved you want to be.

On the recurring side, we have hard costs built into the service (cellular and satellite connectivity). So that 20% of top-line revenue works out to roughly 30% of the underlying margin after those costs come out.

Pro Tip

Don't mark up the monthly TinyPumper service. Clients can look up pricing on their own, and if they see a different number somewhere else, it creates an awkward conversation. Keep the monthly rate the same for everyone. Your upside comes from the 20% recurring share plus whatever you charge for your own service work.

How Billing Works

1

We Collect Everything

All revenue is collected by TinyCo. You don't have to chase payments or deal with invoicing on the TinyPumper side.

2

Payouts Go Out Quarterly

We pay partners once per quarter instead of monthly. The reason is simple: in the oil patch, there's always a gap between when a deal closes, when the gear gets installed, and when the client actually starts paying. Drilling schedules slip, reworks happen, wells get shut in for a bit.

Quarterly payouts let us square everything up first: delayed installs, non-payments, churn, credits, or anything else that needs sorting out. It keeps the accounting clean and accurate for both sides.
