Honest talk: when Sensaphone is the better choice.

TinyPumper isn't the right fit for every operation. If any of these describe you, stick with Sensaphone. Seriously.

1 You need relay/control outputs to remotely switch equipment on/off. The Sentinel PRO has 2 programmable relay outputs. TinyPumper doesn't do remote control — it's monitoring + production management, not a control system.
2 You need 12+ hardwired analog/digital sensors per site. Sensaphone's 12 universal inputs accept any 4-20mA, thermistor, or dry contact sensor. TinyPumper connects sensors via Bluetooth with a 150-foot range.
3 You have a fully configured PLC pulling 64+ Modbus registers and need a pure alarm callout device. The Sentinel PRO's 64-register Modbus capacity makes it a strong PLC companion. TinyPumper supports Modbus but is designed as a standalone platform, not a PLC alarm relay.
4 You're monitoring non-O&G assets. Greenhouses, data centers, HVAC, water treatment — Sensaphone is the better tool. TinyPumper is purpose-built for oil and gas production.

Questions operators ask before switching.

"I already own Sensaphone hardware — is switching wasteful?"
Not necessarily. TinyPumper offers a hybrid approach — we can take over management of your existing monitoring service while you transition. Operators typically see savings when we manage their legacy monitoring compared to what they're paying today. You don't have to strand your investment overnight. Transition at your own pace.
"Sensaphone has been around since 1985 — aren't they more proven?"
In general-purpose monitoring? Absolutely — 40 years, 500,000 units across greenhouses, data centers, HVAC, water treatment. But oil and gas is one of many industries they serve. Greasebook (TinyPumper's parent company) has served independent O&G operators exclusively since 2012 — 600+ operators, 22 states. You want a specialist in your exact use case, not a generalist that also monitors greenhouses.
"What about Sensaphone's relay outputs?"
Fair point. The Sentinel PRO has 2 programmable relay outputs rated for 1A/30VDC. TinyPumper does not offer remote control outputs. If you need to remotely turn equipment on/off, Sensaphone fills that gap. TinyPumper is positioned as monitoring + production management, not a control system. If relay control is a must-have, see our "Honest Talk" section above.
"Is TinyPumper's subscription more expensive?"
It depends what you're comparing. Sensaphone's base Sentinel is $24.95/mo, but that's monitoring-only on ~$1,617 in hardware. The Sentinel PRO is $39.95/mo with ~$1,957 in hardware. TinyPumper at $29/mo includes hardware, sensors, the Greasebook production platform, cellular connectivity, and petroleum engineer support — with no upfront hardware cost. On a 3-year total cost of ownership for 10 wells, TinyPumper is substantially less expensive.
"What if I don't have cell service at my wells?"
That's why we built it the way we did. TinyPumper's multi-carrier SIM auto-switches between Verizon, AT&T, T-Mobile, Sprint, and US Cellular to find the best signal. And when you're truly in the middle of nowhere? Optional satellite connectivity. 100% of the continental US is covered. Sensaphone locks to one carrier at purchase — if that carrier's coverage is weak at your site, you're stuck.
"Can I try TinyPumper without committing?"
Yes. 60-day "Love It or Shove It" money-back guarantee. We pay return shipping. No retention calls, no guilt trips, no hoops. If it doesn't work for your operation, send it back and you're out nothing. Sensaphone does not offer a documented trial period or money-back guarantee.
"What about Sensaphone's 12 hardwired inputs?"
The Sentinel PRO does support 12 universal wired inputs — that's real I/O capacity. TinyPumper connects sensors via Bluetooth (150-foot range) and supports up to 8 sensors per gateway. For most stripper well monitoring — tank levels, pressures, temps — TinyPumper covers the primary use cases. If you're running a complex multi-input PLC integration with 12+ hardwired sensors per site, Sensaphone may be the better hardware fit. But you'll still lack the production software on top.
